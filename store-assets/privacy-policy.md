# Desaturate — Privacy Policy

*Last updated: July 16, 2026*

Desaturate is a Chrome extension that removes color from websites you select,
to reduce compulsive browsing. This policy explains what data the extension
handles.

## What Desaturate stores

The only data Desaturate stores is **the list of website domains you choose to
desaturate** (for example, `facebook.com`). This list is saved using Chrome's
built-in sync storage, which means it lives in your browser and, if you have
Chrome sync enabled, in your own Google account so your settings follow you
across your devices.

## What Desaturate does NOT do

- It does **not** collect, transmit, or sell any data. The extension makes no
  network requests of any kind.
- It does **not** read, record, or analyze the content of pages you visit.
- It does **not** track your browsing history, clicks, or time on site.
- It contains **no analytics, no advertising, and no remote code**.

The developer has no server and receives nothing from your browser. Your site
list is never visible to the developer or to any third party.

## Permissions

- **Storage** — saves your chosen site list, as described above.
- **Scripting** — applies a grayscale stylesheet to the sites you selected,
  and removes it if you deselect a site.
- **Site access** — granted only for sites you explicitly select, via Chrome's
  permission prompt. Desaturate has no access to any other site.

## Removing your data

Uninstalling the extension removes the stored site list. You can also clear it
at any time from the extension's options page by deselecting all sites.
